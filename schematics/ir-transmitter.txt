IR Transmitter Circuit
======================

Components:
- ESP32 GPIO4
- BC547 NPN Transistor
- 100Ω Resistor (R1)
- 10kΩ Resistor (R2)
- 940nm IR LED (5mm)

Schematic:

                    +3.3V
                      │
                      │
                     ╔╧╗
                     ║ ║   IR LED 940nm
                     ║ ║   (anode = top, long leg)
                     ╚╤╝
                      │
                      ├────────── Collector (C)
                      │          BC547 pin 2
                      │
    ESP32 GPIO4 ────┬─┴── R1 (100Ω) ──── Base (B)
                    │                   BC547 pin 1
                    │
                    └── R2 (10kΩ)
                        │
                        GND

                    Emitter (E) ──── GND
                    BC547 pin 3

BC547 Pinout (flat face toward you):

    ┌────────┐
    │  B  C  │
    │        │
    │   E    │
    └────────┘

B = Base     (1) — control from GPIO via 100Ω
C = Collector (2) — connects to IR LED cathode
E = Emitter   (3) — connects to GND

Note: The 10kΩ pull-down resistor between Base and GND
      prevents floating when GPIO is not actively driven.

Operation:
  GPIO HIGH → transistor conducts → IR LED lights
  GPIO LOW  → transistor off → IR LED off

The BC547 acts as a switch, allowing the 3.3V supply
to drive the IR LED with more current than the GPIO pin
could provide directly (~100mA vs ~12mA from GPIO).
